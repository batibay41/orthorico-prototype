A comprehensive guide for orthopedic trainees and surgeons.

Fracture Classification

Intertrochanteric fractures are classified using AO/OTA into types A1, A2, and A3. The Evans classification may also be used.

Mechanism of Injury

Most commonly due to low-energy falls in elderly patients or high-energy trauma in younger patients.

Treatment Algorithm

Treatment depends on fracture stability, patient age, and overall health. Short vs. long intramedullary nails are selected accordingly.

Pre-operative Preparation

Optimize the patient with labs, imaging, and anesthetic evaluation. Address comorbidities and review anticoagulation status.

Instruments & Implants

Includes cephalomedullary nail system, fluoroscopy, traction table, and standard ortho tray. Sponsor-specific implants can be listed.

Positioning

Supine on traction table, contralateral leg flexed. Proper alignment under fluoroscopy is crucial.

Surgical Technique

  • Skin incision
  • Entry point under image guidance
  • Guidewire insertion and reaming
  • Nail insertion and locking screw placement
  • Closure and dressing

Post-operative Protocol

Mobilize with weight-bearing as tolerated. Monitor for complications and schedule follow-up imaging.

Tips & Pearls

  • Confirm entry point in two planes
  • Avoid varus malreduction by correct medial placement
